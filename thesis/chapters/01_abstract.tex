\chapter*{\abstractname}
\label{abstract}

Driven by recent advancements in high-finesse cavities and nanoparticle control, levitodynamics has become a key framework for investigating fundamental physics and developing novel sensors.
We investigate the dynamics of trapped nanoparticles,
    coupled to an optical cavity.
%Due to the coupling of the position of a polarizable particle and the EM field,
%    the equilibrium positions are determined by nonlinear Hamiltonian dynamics.
We develop a theoretical model of the system
    and derived the equilibrium conditions.
%We determine the equilibrium positions numerically
%    and analyze their stability using the Hessian of the Hamiltonian.
Numerical calculations indicate the existence of configurations with multiple equilibrium positions.
We study the system generally with $N$ particles 
    and analyze the $1$ and $2$ particle cases.
For $1$ particle, 
    our analysis indicates that all equilibrium positions are stable,
    within the studied parameter regime.
For $2$ particles we found that the solutions lie on a manifold in configuration space.
